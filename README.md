# IBM HR Analytics – Employee Attrition Analysis

## Problem Statement
Employee attrition is costly for businesses in terms of hiring, training, and lost productivity. This project analyzes IBM's HR dataset to identify the key factors driving employee attrition and generate actionable retention insights.

## Dataset
IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset — 1,470 employee records covering demographics, job role, income, satisfaction scores, and attrition status.

## Tools & Libraries
Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Jupyter Notebook

## Approach
1. **Data Cleaning & Pre-processing** — handled missing values, fixed data types, removed redundant columns
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis & Visualizations** — examined attrition patterns across income, job role, department, and experience
3. **Documentation, Insights & Presentation** — summarized findings into business-ready insights

## Key Findings
- Overtime work is strongly associated with higher attrition
- Employees with lower job satisfaction scores show higher attrition rates
- Attrition is more common among employees with fewer years at the company

## Files
- `Data Cleaning & Pre-processing.ipynb` — data preparation steps
- `EDA & Visualizations.ipynb` — exploratory analysis and charts
- `Documentation, Insights & Presentation.ipynb` — final insights and summary
